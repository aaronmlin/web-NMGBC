The National Medical and General Biology Competition (NMGBC), hosted by AMSA-Universitas Indonesia, is a highly anticipated academic event that challenges and inspires high school students in biology and medicine. Held at Fakultas Kedokteran Universitas Indonesia, this prestigious competition features three main events: t he General & Medical Biology Quiz, the Public Poster Competition, and the Health Speech Competition. More than just a test of knowledge, NMGBC fosters collaboration, critical thinking, and a shared passion for the life sciences, empowering young minds to take a bold step toward becoming future scientists and healthcare professionals.

NMGBC 2025 dives into the critical theme of Cardiovascular Health, highlighting its vital role in overall well-being. With cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) as the leading global cause of death —claiming 17.9 million lives annually, including 650,000 in Indonesia—the urgency for awareness and prevention has never been greater. Indonesia faces the highest CVD burden in Southeast Asia , ranking fifth globally in CVD mortality.

 

Through this year’s competition, NMGBC not only raises awareness but also empowers high school students to deepen their understanding of biology and medicine, fostering a competitive yet inspiring environment where they can develop the knowledge and skills to tackle global health challenges and shape the future of healthcare.

It consists of three rounds: the preliminary round, the semifinal round, and the final round. Each round will test participants’ abilities in all fields of speech

The Public Poster Competition at the National Medical and General Biology Competition (NMGBC) 2025 is a national-level public poster competition for high school students. Public posters serve as a creative mass media tool to educate the public and improve healthcare quality in Indonesia. The competition consists of three stages: Preliminary, Semifinal, and Final, using a hybrid format.
